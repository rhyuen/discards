A fun tidbit mentioned in the article the 'ch' unit is based on the '0' character. It then follows that 65 ch is equal in length to 65 '0' characters.

I thought the select .wrapper > * {grid-column: 2;} was a really elegant solution to having all the immediate children in the main tag centred in the document.

The research tidbit on "Wildcard Permorance" was also nice to know. I didn't know that using the * selector in css was considered a bad practice by developers. Now, I know that it isn't!

The mention of calc() being able to do math with different kinds of units was something I didn't know. However, the example he demonstrated with calc(100% - 32px) didn't work as expected on this page so I just used padding.

The document constrains the width of the document to 65ch or 100%, whichever is the lesser of the two because having too many characters on one line is sub-optimal for the reading experience. Images, however, are under no such constraint, hence they they extend the width of the document in all circumstances.

It turns out that the 'figure' tag has margins by default.
